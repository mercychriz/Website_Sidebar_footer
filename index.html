African Renaissance Monument

The African Renaissance Monument is a 52-meter tall bronze statue located on top of one of the twin hills known as Collines des Mamelles, outside Dakar, Senegal. The monument was built to celebrate the rebirth of Africa and its cultural and historical heritage. The idea for the monument was first proposed by Senegal's former President, Abdoulaye Wade, in the year 2000. The President had a vision of creating a landmark that would symbolize the rebirth of Africa and its cultural and historical heritage. He wanted the monument to be a symbol of unity and hope for a brighter future for the continent. The monument depicts a man, woman, and child emerging from a volcano and was designed by the Senegalese architect Pierre Goudiaby. The statue was built by Mansudae Overseas Projects, a company from North Korea. The monument was officially opened to the public in 2010, and it was unveiled in front of 19 African heads of state, including President of Malawi and the African Union, Bingu wa Mutharika, Jean Ping of the African Union Commission, and the Presidents of Benin, Cape Verde, Republic of the Congo, Ivory Coast, The Gambia, Liberia, Mali, Mauritania, and Zimbabwe, as well as representatives from North Korea, and Jesse Jackson and musician Akon, both from the United States, all of whom were given a tour. click for more Info
